Notes

  • Method of Sec. 22.3.5–22.3.8: the weld is treated as a line with length but no area. Standard design formulas (Table 22.2) then give a unit force f in kips per linear inch of weld instead of a stress, which is compared with the allowable unit force of the weld to find the required size or capacity.
  • Allowable weld shear = 0.30 × nominal tensile strength of filler metal (Table 22.4), acting on the effective throat 0.707 ω. Shear on the adjacent base metal is limited to 0.40 × Fy × t.
  • Where more than one load acts, unit forces are combined vectorially at the same point on the joint (Sec. 22.3.6). The worksheet checks the extreme top and bottom corners of the weld group and reports the governing point.
  • AWS D1.1 minimum fillet sizes (by thickness of thicker part joined): t ≤ ¼″ → ⅛″, ≤ ½″ → 3/16″, ≤ ¾″ → ¼″, > ¾″ → 5/16″. Along an edge of material ≥ ¼″ thick, ωmax = t − 1/16″.
  • Printing corrections (†) applied to the CRC reproduction of Table 22.3, verified by direct line-integration of each weld group: the centroid distances print with an extra power (e.g. “Nx = d³/2(b+d)”) — dimensional consistency requires d²; shape 4 (L) bottom Sw = d²(4b+d)/[6(2b+d)] (the table repeats the channel expression, which does not satisfy Sw = Ix/c); shape 6 (⊓) Jw second term is d²(b+d)²/(b+2d) (printed with b²). For shape 8 the published table repeats shape 6’s Jw, which includes a b²d/2 flange-offset term the centered stem welds do not have; the worksheet shows the published value but uses the corrected (smaller, conservative) Jw for torsional capacity. Shape 13’s Sw is used exactly as published.
  • Sign convention: P, V, M, T magnitudes are combined additively (worst case). Units: inches, kips, ksi. For guidance only — final designs must be verified by a licensed engineer against the governing code.
